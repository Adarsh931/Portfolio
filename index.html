Summer Research Intern

Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, India

Title: Understanding the functional role of hsp31 mini-family
Guide: Dr. Patrick D'Silva, Gautam Susarla (Mentor)
Background: The aim of the project was to understand role of hsp31 mini family in maintaining cellular reactive oxygen species(ROS) homeostasis and in deglycation of DNA and protein in Saccharomyces cerevisiae BY4741.
• To check the phenotype of the knocked out strains available and generated, spot assay was done at different temperatures as well as checked via oxidative stress involving H2O2 (Sigma) and methylglyoxal(MG) in YPD plates. FACS was also performed to confirm the same using H2DCFDA and DHE stains, to check the levels of overall ROS and super oxides respectively.
• DNA fragment was treated with methyl glyoxal (MG) and purified glycated DNA was subjected for Nae1 digestion. DNA was visualized in agarose gel for investigation. Purified glycated DNA was also subjected for gel retardation assay which involved running the glycated DNA in agarose gel along with untreated DNA.
• Further study was done to see whether hsp31 could rescue cell, DNA and protein from forming Advance glycated end (AGE) products using SDS-PAGE and western blot.

May - July, 2018

Winter Research Intern

All India Institute of Medical Science (AIIMS), New Delhi, India

Title: Understanding severity of malaria parasite (Plasmodium species) from inpatients
Guide: Dr. Pragyan Acharya, Atreyi Pramanik (Mentor)
Background: The aim of the project was to understand the effect of malaria parasite (Plasmodium species) from blood sample of inpatients as compared to the last year and maintain proper data sheet for that.
• Extracted DNA from blood samples of patients admitted in All India Institute of Medical Science(AIIMS) Hospital
• Did PCR for 105 inpatients for 5 different species of Plasmodium namely falciparum, vivax, malariae, ovale and knowlesi.

Title: Designing single common DNA primer for AFI causing viruses in humans
Guide: Dr. Pragyan Acharya, Atreyi Pramanik (Mentor)
Background: Aim was to design common DNA primer for acute febrile illness (AFI) causing viruses in humans for easy and fast diagnosis by PCR technique.
• Classified AFI viruses according to Baltimore classification and Extracted gene sequence of polymerase in respective viruses.
• Did Multiple Locus Sequence Alignment (MLSA) in these viruses using Clustal Omega program to find common sequence.
• Finally, did BLAST on NCBI website to find whether these common sequence is present in human genome or not.
